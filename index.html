Al-Kabsa - A Main Dish In Saudia Arabia

"Al-Kabsa" is a popular Saudi Arabian dish that is mainly composed of rice, minced meat, chicken or small pieces of meat, along with onions, garlic, and various spices such as cardamom, saffron, cumin, black pepper, and coriander. Typically, Al-Kabsa is served in a large pot on the table and decorated with nuts, raisins, and fried onions. The components and preparation of Al-Kabsa can vary from region to region in Saudi Arabia, and it is considered a main dish for occasions, holidays, and large gatherings. Al-Kabsa is a popular and delicious traditional dish that is enjoyed by citizens and visitors alike.

Modus operandi of "Umm Ali" bread

"Um Ali" is a popular Saudi Arabian dessert that is similar to bread pudding. It is made by layering pieces of puff pastry or phyllo dough with nuts such as pistachios, almonds, and walnuts, as well as raisins and sometimes shredded coconut. The layers are then soaked in a sweet, creamy mixture of milk, cream, and sugar, and baked until golden brown and crispy on top. Um Ali is a rich and delicious dessert that is often served during special occasions and celebrations in Saudi Arabia. The name "Um Ali" means "Ali's mother" in Arabic, and according to legend, the dessert was named after a woman who was famous for her hospitality and love for cooking.

How to make Al-Marqooq

"Al-Marqooq" is a popular dish in Saudi and Gulf cuisine that consists of thin layers of dough saturated with oil, which are then browned to a golden and crispy texture. Al-Marqooq is usually served as a light snack with tea or coffee, and can be eaten alone or with hummus or salty cheese dip. Al-Marqooq is prepared in homes and pastry shops throughout Saudi Arabia and the Gulf, and is especially common during the holy month of Ramadan when it is served during the suhoor or iftar meals. The ingredients and spices used to make Al-Marqooq can vary depending on the region and local traditions, and it can be prepared in different shapes and sizes.
